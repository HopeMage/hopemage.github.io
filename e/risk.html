Risk

Introduction

This page discusses the important topic of risk.

Definition

Risk is a type of potential for an outcome, generally of negative consequences.
Being aware of risk, containing it, and managing the response to risk
are key to success, or at least avoiding failure.

Classification

Risks are often categorized by severity and probability.
The most urgent risks are both very severe and probable,
while the least worrisome are minor and unlikely.

In between the most and least alarming risks is a range of intermediary
risks with varying combinations of severities and probabilities.

They can also be categorized by detectability.
A hidden risk that is not very detectable can be more dangerous,
even if the severity and/or probability are only moderate.

Management

Dealing with risks involves multiple steps:
identification, analysis, prioritization, and mitigation.
