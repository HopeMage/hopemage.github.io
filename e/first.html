First Principles

Introduction

This page outlines the motivational foundations of the Hope Action Plan.

Motivations

Basic ethics and morality teach us to care about the « well-being » of others,
which means their general health and happiness.

The opposite of well-being is « suffering »
of which there is simply too much still in the world.
This general suffering must be reduced in order to improve global well-being.

The overarching hope is to meaningfully improve the overall well-being of the world.
Everything that follows is simply to fulfill that hope.

Expansion

Prioritizing the reduction of suffering is sometimes called « negative utilitarianism ».
Another related philosophy is « effective altruism »
which aims to do the most good per resource consumed.

However, suffering is only the opening step and not the sole concern.
Despite the current level of development, human civilization still has much unfulfilled potential.
We must look not just to reducing suffering but also far beyond it to fulfilling the potential of humanity.
